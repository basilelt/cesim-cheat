## 2. Main Objective and Winning Criteria

The main objective is to deliver sustainable, profitable growth. Typically, this is measured by cumulative total return to shareholders, combining share price development and dividends.

### Best Practices for Teamwork

- Appoint a CEO each round to coordinate and submit final decisions (rotate the role).
- Use the Team Forum to exchange ideas.
- Agree on an internal deadline prior to the actual deadline.

## 3. Market Outlook and Demand

Read the Market outlook for trends and parameters before making decisions. Quantitative parameters (such as tax rates) are usually accurate forecasts, while market growth can vary based on competitor actions.

Demand is determined in three steps:

- Total market size: Based on economic conditions, average price levels, promotional budgets, and technological evolution.
- Technology market share: Affected by price levels, promotion, number of competitors offering it, network coverage, and attractiveness.
- Company market share: Affected by your price, promotion, features, previous market share, and public image (ESG).

## 4. Production

Global allocation of production across the USA and Asia is critical.

- Unit costs: Determined by basic cost levels, the U-shaped production cost function, learning curve (cumulative global production), carbon taxes, and energy/water usage.
- Sustainability and ESG: Renewable energy costs more but improves public image. Lower energy/water consumption per unit strengthens environmental perception. The EU's CBAM framework imposes additional carbon taxes on imports.
- Inventory: Handled via FIFO. Oldest products are sold first without depreciation. Inventory costs include holding and capital opportunity costs.
- Investments: You can invest in or divest plants. Pay attention to cash flows and ensure return on investment (ROI) justifies plant depreciation and financing costs.

## 5. Human Resources and R&D

- Staffing: Manage R&D engineers, turnover, and budgets. Turnover decreases with higher wages and training.
- Efficiency: Efficient R&D lowers person-days required to develop technologies in-house.
- Training and labor policies: Prioritizing training (for example, basic training and work safety) and setting progressive labor policies impact R&D efficiency, turnover, and ESG public image.
- R&D execution: Mix in-house development (1 round delay) and license purchasing (immediate availability) to upgrade technology and features.

## 6. Marketing and Logistics

- Marketing mix: Decide on price, promotion, and features per market. You can adopt specific focuses (Low price, Features, Sustainability, Brand) that amplify success or failure of your actual metrics.
- After-sales: Offering extended warranties generates demand. Data collection options create new revenue streams but can harm public image if data is not protected.
- Logistics: Set supply priorities to favor markets with the highest unit margins in case of shortages. Transportation incurs base costs plus flat/ad valorem tariffs and CO2 emissions.

## 7. Taxation and Finance

- Transfer pricing: Move profits between units to take advantage of varying corporate tax rates or to cover fixed local costs. Taxes are paid locally.
- Financing decisions: Made through the USA parent company. Manage long-term debt, share issuance, buybacks, and dividends.
- Capital structure: Keep the equity ratio between 40-60%. Buy back shares or issue dividends if excess cash exists.
- Short-term debt: A minimum of 2 million USD cash must be maintained; falling below this triggers expensive, automatic short-term debt.

## 8. Calculation of Key Financial Ratios

### Cumulative Total Shareholder Return (p.a.), %

$$
100\% \times \left[
\frac{\text{Share price at end of round} + \text{cumulative dividends per share} + \text{interest for dividend}}{\text{First round share price}} - 1
\right]
$$

### Return on Sales (ROS), %

$$
\frac{\text{Profit for the round}}{\text{Sales revenue}}
$$

### Equity Ratio, %

$$
\frac{\text{Shareholder's equity}}{\text{Total assets}}
$$

### Net Debt to Equity (Gearing), %

$$
\frac{\text{Interest-bearing liabilities (short and long-term)} - \text{Cash and cash equivalents}}{\text{Shareholder's equity}}
$$

### Return on Capital Employed (ROCE), %

$$
\frac{\text{Operating profit (EBIT)}}{\text{Average shareholders' equity} + \text{Average interest-bearing liabilities}}
$$

### Return on Equity (ROE), %

$$
\frac{\text{Profit for the round}}{\text{Average shareholders' equity}}
$$

### Earnings per Share (EPS), USD

$$
\frac{\text{Profit for the round}}{\text{Shares outstanding at the end of round}}
$$

### Dividend Yield, %

$$
\frac{\text{Dividend per share}}{\text{Share price at the end of round}}
$$

### P/E Ratio

$$
\frac{\text{Share price at the end of round}}{\text{Earnings per share (EPS)}}
$$

### Gross Margin, %

$$
\frac{\text{Sales revenue} - \text{Total costs of unit sold}}{\text{Sales revenue}}
$$
